About Kidogo

What we do

Kidogo is the largest childcare network in Kenya and it operates a small number of ‘centers of excellence’ across Nairobi. The Kidogo Way is a proprietary approach to promoting young children's healthy growth & development. This framework guides their training, curriculum, and quality assurance for caregivers and centres in the network.

Why we do it

Every day, thousands of working mothers in East Africa’s informal settlements drop off their young children at unlicensed and congested “babycare” centers. Without good supportive care in their early years, these children face physical and learning disabilities in school. In addition, most of the women being single mothers are unable to hold consistent employment, as they lack reliable childcare services during the working day.

How we do it

By joining the Kidogo Network, Mamapreneurs receive intensive training & mentoring on early childhood care & education, entrepreneurship, and health & nutrition to improve the quality of services offered to young children. Mompreneurs are also provided with a starter kit including key resources for their centers, and ongoing quality assurance to maintain Kidogo’s minimum standards.

Who we serve

Kidogo supports a variety of workplaces (factories, farms, and offices) to implement childcare solutions that help support their workforce, and promote women’s labor force participation. Kidogo identifies, train, and support female entrepreneurs (Mamapreneurs) to start or grow their own childcare micro-businesses in their local communities.

Impact Management Strategy


At Kidogo, we focus on making impact measurement a central element of our decision-making process. Our goals in providing a service that works at the intersection of supporting female entrepreneurs and child development have been to:

  • Make early childhood care and education affordable
  • Support a variety of workplaces to implement childcare solutions
  • Promote young children's health and development
  • Encourage women's labor-force participation
We're taking measures to line with the objectives by requesting the support of the Miller Center for Social Entrepreneurship at Santa Clara University and Sopact. We've partnered up on analyzing the data from our core business model to see how we can improve from there.

Quality Assurance

Quality assurance is done to maintain Kidogo's minimum standards through an assessment covering various factors that contribute to a mamapreneurs' growth and a child's development, such as Safety, Health, Nutrition, Learning,.. total 8 factors.

This is scored out of 20 and a quality level zone is assigned to it:
Red is for centers that score 0-7 points, Yellow is for centers that score 8 - 16 points and Green is 17 - 20 points.

At the begining, it showed that the majority of the daycare centers are under Yellow Score (73.77%) no matter in which types of the centers. Red Score(18.03%) is mostly among Home based and School based. There are fewer centers achieved Green Score (8.20%) and center based is doing better than other types.
